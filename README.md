# HUBRIS: Helpful Utility to Bring Reporting to Spreadsheets

This project is my response to the frustrations of working with
organisations that insist on managing their businesses, or major
aspects of it, using spreadsheets.
It's particularly frustrating
when organisations use entirely different architectures
in their internal and public-facing systems.

Many articles have been written on the limitations of spreadsheet
technology - if you have any doubts then look at the "The Problem
with Spreadsheets" section of this
[LinkedIn article](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/spreadsheets-inadequate-effective-management--gjsse/).

Rather than try to change the way people do business (although I
should like to do that, my powers are way too limited).
It's good to know that large organisations are
[providing advice](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/creating-and-sharing-spreadsheets))
— although in many cases better advice might be:
_stop using spreadsheets_!

Rather than continuing to upset my colleagues
by bitching and moaning
I decided that I would try
to help people utilise that locked-up data
more effectively.

HUBRIS allows you to add reporting data selection details
to any existing spreadsheet.
The reporting details are used
to extract data
from the spreadsheet
in a form that can be injected
into a template
to produce human-readable output
that is easier to digest
than raw data in a spreadsheet.
